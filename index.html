Frequently Asked Questions

How do I play Powerball Australia? +

Choose seven numbers from 1 to 35, plus one Powerball number from 1 to 20. Buy your ticket online or at an authorised retailer before the weekly Thursday night draw. Match all winning numbers to score the Division 1 jackpot—there are also eight other prize divisions, so there are many ways to win!

Do I need to verify my account before buying tickets? +

Yes. Australian law requires you to verify your identity when registering online. This is a quick process and helps keep your account secure and your winnings safe. You’ll need valid identification, such as a driver’s licence or passport.

How do I check if I’ve won a prize? +

After each Thursday draw, results are published on our website. Simply log in to your account or visit the “Recent Results” section to see the winning numbers and check your tickets. Winners are also notified directly by email or SMS.

How can I claim my Powerball winnings? +

If you play online, all prizes up to a certain amount are credited automatically to your account. Larger wins may require additional verification and direct bank transfer. If you bought your ticket in-store, take your winning ticket to any authorised retailer or follow the instructions on the back for larger prizes.

What are the odds of winning a Powerball prize? +

Powerball Australia offers nine prize divisions. Your chance of winning any prize is 1 in 44 per standard game. The odds of taking out Division 1 are 1 in 134,490,400, but every ticket is a chance to win—especially with multiple entry options.

Can I play Powerball if I’m not in Australia? +

To play Powerball Australia online, you must have a valid Australian address and meet age and ID requirements. If you’re overseas, you can only participate through authorised agents with eligible Australian credentials.

What is a PowerHit or System Entry? +

A PowerHit entry guarantees your ticket has every possible Powerball number, giving you more chances to win Division 1. System entries let you choose more than seven numbers, covering every combination for even more ways to win across all prize divisions.

Is there a minimum age to play Powerball? +

Yes. You must be at least 18 years old to buy Powerball tickets or participate in any lottery in Australia. Always play responsibly!

How are Powerball jackpots determined? +

Powerball jackpots start at $4 million and grow each week there is no Division 1 winner. Jackpots can climb rapidly, with record prizes exceeding $100 million!

How do Powerball syndicates work? +

A syndicate lets you and others pool your money to buy more tickets as a group, increasing your chances of winning. Any prize is split between syndicate members, making it a popular, social way to play.
